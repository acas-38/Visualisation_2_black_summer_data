The Black Summer bushfires are widely regarded as one of the most catastrophic bushfire seasons in Australian history. Spanning from September 2019 to February 2020, the fires devastated vast areas across multiple states and territories. Thousands of separate blazes ignited throughout this period, burning millions of hectares of land, destroying homes, displacing communities, and severely impacting native ecosystems and wildlife populations.
